    At some point, everyone needs some help and support and that can come from a variety of resources. If you feel that you can't talk to a friend, family member or responsible adult who knows you, I've listed below the contact details of some mental health charities targeted.

    Feeling suicidal? Click here for information on how to get help immediately

    The Mix

    I chose The Mix because they offer a wide range of services targeted specifically at young people up to the age of 25.

    "The Mix is the UK's leading support service for young people. We are here to help you take on any challenge you're facing - from mental health to comey, from homelessness to finding a job, from break-ups to drugs. Talk to us via online, social or our free confidential helpline."

    You can call The Mix via freephone on 0808 808 4994

    For more information visit their website at: www.themix.org.uk

    Click here if you want to make a donation and support The Mix.

    YoungMinds

    YoungMinds is a fantastic charity offering support and information to not only children and adolescents, but also parents. They providing mental health training on a wide range of topics.

    YoungMinds "champions children and young people's mental health and wellbeing across the UK. Driven by their experience we create change so that children and young people can cope with life's adversities, find help when needed and succeed in life."

    To find out more about YoungMinds, visit them at: youngminds.org.uk

    Click here if you want to make a donation and support YoungMinds.

    Mind

    Mind is a broad and far reaching charity, offering support to all ages and demographics

    Mind "provides advice and support to empower anyone experiencing a mental health problem. We campaign to improve services, raise awareness and promote understanding."

    Samaritans (Central London)

    Samaritans is a charity which is at the forefront of campaining, access and assistance. Their services are well known for confidentiality and 24 hour access.

    "Anyone can contact Samaritans. We offer a safe place for you to talk any time you like, in your own way, no matter how old you are. Samaritans' primary responsibility is to you, not your parents or guardian."
